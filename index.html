Staten Island Sewer System Problems Continue Despite Upgrades

Even with the completion of the Blue Belt project, Staten Island's sewer complaints are increasing more than ever.

by Laura Bratton

November 12, 2021

After years of complaints, the city finally funded the completion of the Blue Belt, which created natural wetland areas to improve stormwater drainage in Staten Island. The project was completed in 2021, after years of advocacy by Staten Island residents in the wake of Hurricane Sandy's destruction, rising populations, and continued overdevelopment in the area.

But even these efforts haven't helped. With record breaking rains from Hurricane Ida, Staten Island again experienced issues with sewer system backflows.

Between 2020 and 2021, Staten Island saw some of the greatest increases in sewer complaints per population to the city Department of Environmental Protection.

Melissa Checker, a professor of Urban Studies at Queens College, says she's not surprised by the numbers.

"The Blue Belt was never going to solve all the problemts out there."

Checker says Staten Island residents still need a lot of infrastructure to support population increases that have happened over the last couple of decades.
